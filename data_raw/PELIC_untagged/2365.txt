When surnames began appearing in Europe 800 years ago a person's identity and occupation were often interviewed. To illustrate a surname was a direct link between who a person was and what the person did. For instant Taylor is the Old English spelling of tailor and Clark is derived from clerk an occupation of considerable status during the Middle Ages because it required literacy. The names Walker Wright Carter Stewart and Turner indicate occupations. To illustrate that a walker was someone who cleaned cloth while the wright was a carpenter or metalworker. However a carter was someone who drove a cart and stewart was a person in charge of a farm or estate while a turner worked a lathe. On the other hand one of the few occupational surnames reflecting the work of women is Webster which refers to a female weaver
