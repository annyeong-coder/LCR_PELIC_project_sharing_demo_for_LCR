By LAKIESHA R. CARR Published March 14 2007 Times A 17 year old student Mary Masterman invented something new called spectrograph. It is a device that identifies characteristics of molecules and is really expensive. She spent only 300 to make it up and won the top prize in a competition in Oklahoma City. The top prize was 100 000 scholarship. It took 6 months for her to make it work 3 months to build and 3 months to function properly. 1700 Students applied this year's contest there were 40 finalists at the end who won a laptop at the end. New York had 12 finalists. The second place winner project was the unfolding of simple closed curves and the third one was mathematics project exploring loop homology . Students were eliminated by enthusiasm and versatility in the sciences. The president of Science Service said winners had a certain spark.
