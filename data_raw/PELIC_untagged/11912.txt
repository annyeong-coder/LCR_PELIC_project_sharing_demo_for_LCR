Libyan cuisine is vary from north libya and south libya but generally it is considered as a mixture of Arabic and Italian food. Particularly macaroni. But a famous local dish is couscous which is a boiled cereal traditionally millet now fairly often wheat in combination with meat and potatoes. Libya also grows olives and lots of fruit and vegetables. Sharba is a traditional Libyan soup. Bazin is a hard paste made from barley salt and water. All alcoholic drink is banned in Libya and alcohol is considered as illegal and impolite. Libyan tea is a thick beverage served in a small glass often accompanied by mint or peanuts.
