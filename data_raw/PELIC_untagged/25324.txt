THE FESTIVAL OF MARDI GRAS Caribbean culture is full of musical and religious celebrations. The Festival of Mardi Gras Shrove Day which takes place Tuesday prior to Easter every years is one of them. Initially French caribbean slaves celebrated the day to start the new year. They believed that bad spirits must be hunted out of the town. Nowaday people have turned this day to a festival day and hunt bad spirits out off the city by making a lof sounds with musical intruments. On that day families reunite and make wishes. Preparation begins two to three weeks before the holiday and finishes in 'apotheose' with a great musical parade across all the city. To get ready for this celebration many things must be prepared and done. First folk bands execute choregraphies and repeat songs. Next people buy rich clothes to make costumes to wear and masks to disguise the day of carnival. Houses are decorated and paint sometimes with rich and various colors. The day of the festival local merchants play an important role. They build rich and colorfull stall display on them their marchandises which consist ofcolorful flowers fruits fireworks and lanterns. People buy these goods for themselves for children and for their relatives. A the sunset misicians folk bands with their orchestra and dancers begin the procession playing all kind of music jazz samba traditionnal musics etc to make a lot of sounds. People sing and dance from the afternoon until midnight. At midnight the effigy of Vaval representation of the bad spirits is burned. After that celebrations continue all the night to early morning hours. There are many kinds of activities and people enjoy in all the city. The festival of Mardi Gras also begins the celebration of the holy week that ends at Easter the day of Jesus redemption. Caribbean must be visited at this period to enjoy the wide variety of music foods and people.
