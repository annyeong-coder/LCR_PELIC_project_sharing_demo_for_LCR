Reporter he asked Marie she how she had become the Queen of France. She answered that when she had been fourteen her mother had told her she had had to go to France to live in the French court and she hadn't known the fact that she would have to marry Louis. He asked how she felt about it then. She responded that she loved Paris and it was so lively and beautiful there compared to Germany. He asked what she said to the poor who were complaining that they had no food to eat. She commented that she said let them eat cake. He asked whether she thought that they would react well to that statement. She replied that she didn't care what they thought and was queen.
