There have many kinds of pressures of being a student not only for students who study in their hometown but also for students who study abroad. They have pressures from parents applying to undergraduate or graduate school and having a high paid job in the future. The pressure from parents is the most common pressure with students who study abroad. Most of the students don't have a job while they are studying so their parents need to offer them money to live buy food clothes and pay rent for life and books that needed for school. Those things cost a lot. Therefore parents would like their children to finish their education as fast as they can. Applying to undergraduate or graduate school also has a lot of pressure. Most of the students would like to study in a high level school which means they need to spend lots of time for studying taking quizzes and tests so that they can attend the school they want. Having a high paid job in the future has lots of pressure too. Before you get one you need to finish the study and probably need to get a master degree. Otherwise you will not be accepted by a well known company. It is also important for people who want to get married and have a family. If you don't have a job then you can not have the ability to support your family. It is true that students have many pressures They need to take care of their grades and make sure they can have a high score on the exams. They also need to clear their mind to decide what they are going to be in the future.
