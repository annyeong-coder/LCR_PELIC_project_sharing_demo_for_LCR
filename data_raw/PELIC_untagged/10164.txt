ANON_NAME_0 McCrae and his colleagues studied real and perceived personalities in roughly 50 countries and found that cultural stereotypes cannot reflect the average personality of real members of that culture. His team began with two groups of NEO PI R surveys they had previously collected in a wide variety of countries. The researchers also conducted a third survey in about 50 countries using questions about the same 30 characteristics but in this survey they asked the volunteers to describe a typical person from their culture.
