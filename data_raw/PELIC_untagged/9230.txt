The following is the content of a recently found conversation note between a reporter and Marie Antoinette the wife of King Louis XVI of France. The reporter asked Marie how she had become the Queen of France. She answered that when she was fourteen her mother had told her to go to France to live in the French court. At that time she hadn't known she would have to marry Louis XIV thought. The report continued to ask her how she felt about the change. She replied that she loved Paris because it was so lively and beautiful there compared to Germany. The reporter noted that the poor were complaining that they had no food to eat. Then the reporter asked Marie what she said to this. She commented to let them eat cake. The reporter also asked whether they would react well to her statement. She claimed that she didn't care what they thought for she was the queen.
