Title of Article A country idyll Date June 27 Auther Marylynn Uricchio Soruce Pittsburgh quarterly Summary Roger Ferri was an architectural star whose name was often preceded by the word visionary . The residence he designed was traditional country house which is used rough hewn stone wood and copper. He had been working with designer Louis Talotta of Parke Interiors in Pittsburgh on the interior design before he died from AIDS in 1991. They worked very well together. After Ferri's death Talotta integrated his work into the original structure echoing Ferri's design. Talotta continues to work updating as needed but keeping his styles. Vocabulary 1. Word visionary Part of Speech nown Definition able to make plans for the future that are imaginative and wise 2. Word incongruity Part of Speech nown Definition strange or not suitable thing for a particular situation
